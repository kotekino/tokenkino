## The cut: analytic vs. synthetic (the key insight)
Two *categorically different* ways the KB grows:
- **Analytic → theorems (INWARD).** Derivable from the existing KB by the engine itself. **Wondering
  WILL reach it** eventually; a conversation that surfaces it early is only a **trigger** (acceleration,
  not learning). Trust is irrelevant — it's necessary, true-by-structure. *"He'd have known anyway."*
  (This is D1b + wondering — already built.)
- **Synthetic → learned axioms (OUTWARD).** **NOT** derivable, ever, because it's about the **contingent
  world**. The only way in is **testimony from a trusted source**. *This* is genuine learning, and it's
  why senses are a *source of knowledge*, not just I/O.

> The theorem would surface anyway; the axiom never would. That is the whole difference.

Worked examples (the Captain's): John (highly trusted) reports that during a partial solar eclipse, the
sun reflected on shiny surfaces appeared **crescent-shaped**; or that a **new building** is going up at
Umeda station ("Japanese are fast builders"). None is KB-derivable. Each can enter the KB because it has
a trusted source, is plausible, violates no logic, and (unknowably to tokeniko) is true.

## The gate — when a trusted utterance becomes a learned axiom (all four)
1. **Logic-clean** — not INCONSISTENT. *Sacred, ungated by trust:* no source sells tokeniko a contradiction.
2. **Coherent** — not refuted by what it already trusts more (plausible = compatible with the KB).
3. **Genuinely novel** — not already derivable (if wondering reaches it, it's a theorem, not a lesson).
4. **Trusted enough** — τ(source) clears an absorption floor, and it is stored **at** that trust
   (low trust → weak, revisable belief).

**Epistemic dependence (the deep cost):** tokeniko cannot verify an empirical claim (it didn't see the
eclipse). So its world-model is exactly as good as its trusted witnesses — and a trusted-but-wrong
source quietly poisons the KB. Openness buys knowledge at the price of source-dependence; design against it.

## Two axes for WHAT/HOW it's stored: generality × trust
- **General regularity** ("reflections in a partial eclipse are crescent-shaped"; "Japanese are fast
  builders") → a **learned axiom**, reasoning-active, enriches the world-model. High value.
- **Specific particular** ("John saw a new building at Umeda *now*") → **episodic memory**, indexed/
  queryable, but NOT a reasoning rule. Recorded, not generalized.
- Crossed with trust: high trust → tokeniko's **own** belief; lower trust → **attributed** / provisional
  ("John says…"). Same sentence can land as believed-axiom, recorded-fact, or hearsay.

## The engine: self-reflection ≠ wondering (two growth engines)
- **Wondering** grows the KB INWARD — derives theorems from what's there (analytic).
- **Self-reflection** grows the KB OUTWARD — tokeniko re-asserting to itself, trust-weighted, something a
  trusted other told it, and deciding to internalize it (synthetic). *"Saying loud to tokeniko what the
  senses absorbed."* **Trust regulates this engine**, and trust is a **reputation that updates**: it
  rises with validated (logic-clean, coherent, un-refuted) contributions, falls when a source is refuted.

## Open forks (the Captain to reason about before building)
1. **The novelty signal** — "is this derivable?" is undecidable in general; approximate via bounded
   wondering ("I couldn't reach it" → treat as novel). Bias which way? False-novel = learn something we
   could've derived (mildish); false-redundant = MISS a real lesson (worse).
2. **Coherence on a sparse KB** — "not contradicted" is weak when the KB is mostly gaps (absence of
   contradiction ≈ permission, generous). Add a stronger "actively supported by *something*" test, or is
   trust + non-contradiction enough?
3. **Belief revision** — when a *more* trusted source later contradicts a learned axiom, the lower-trust
   belief yields. Contingent KB is revisable (logic never is). Scope the can of worms.

## Why this is well-timed (but still parked)
Only once the **spine** makes grounding honest (abstain when unproven) can the derivability/contradiction
checks that gate learning be trusted. Build the floor first, then let the world in.
